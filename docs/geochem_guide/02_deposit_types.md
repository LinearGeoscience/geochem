# Geochemical Signatures of Deposit Types

## 1. Porphyry Systems (Cu-Au-Mo)
Large tonnage, low-to-medium grade deposits associated with calc-alkaline magmatism.

### Geochemical Zonation
- **Core (Potassic Zone)**: Cu, Au, Mo. High K/Na ratios (biotite, K-feldspar).
- **Proximal (Phyllic Zone)**: Cu, Mo, Pyrite. High Al, K, S. Depletion of Na, Ca, Mg.
- **Intermediate (Argillic)**: Zn, Pb, Mn halo ("Zinc Doughnut").
- **Distal (Propylitic)**: As, Sb, Li, Tl, Te. Broad halo useful for vectoring.
- **Vertical Zonation**:
    - Deep: Cu-Mo-Au
    - Shallow: As-Sb-Hg-Li (Lithocap signature)

### Pathfinder Elements
- **Primary**: Cu, Au, Mo, Ag.
- **Pathfinders**: As, Sb, Bi, Te, Se, Li, Tl.
- **Fertility Indices (Zircon/Apatite)**:
    - High Eu/Eu* (>0.4) in zircon (Oxidized magma).
    - High S and Cl in apatite.

## 2. Epithermal Systems (Au-Ag)
Shallow, hydrothermal systems often genetically linked to porphyries.

### High Sulphidation (HS)
- **Fluids**: Acidic, oxidized, magmatic-dominant.
- **Alteration**: Vuggy silica (leached core) -> Advanced Argillic (Alunite, Pyrophyllite) -> Argillic.
- **Geochemistry**:
    - Enriched: Au, Ag, Cu, As, Sb, Bi, Sn.
    - Depleted: Mn, Zn, Pb (often leached).
    - Volatiles: High S, Hg.

### Low Sulphidation (LS)
- **Fluids**: Near-neutral, reduced, meteoric-dominant.
- **Alteration**: Adularia-Sericite (Potassic), Bladed Calcite (Boiling).
- **Geochemistry**:
    - Enriched: Au, Ag, As, Sb, Hg, Tl, Se.
    - Vertical Zonation: Base metals (Pb, Zn, Cu) deep -> Au-Ag (Boiling Zone) -> Hg-Sb-As (Steam Heated/Sinter).
    - **Key Ratio**: Au/Ag is typically lower in LS than HS.

## 3. Volcanogenic Massive Sulphide (VMS)
Submarine exhalative deposits forming lenses of massive sulphide.

### Alteration Pipes (Footwall)
- **Core**: Chlorite rich (High Fe, Mg). Intense Na, Ca depletion.
- **Halo**: Sericite rich (High K, Al).
- **Indices**:
    - **Ishikawa Alteration Index (AI)**: $(K_2O + MgO) / (K_2O + MgO + Na_2O + CaO) * 100$. High values (>90) indicate intense chlorite-sericite alteration.
    - **CCPI (Chlorite-Carbonate-Pyrite Index)**: Measures Fe-Mg metasomatism.

### Exhalites & Lithogeochemistry
- **Exhalites**: Fe-Mn-Si rich horizons (chert, BIF) marking the ore horizon. High Ba, Zn, Cu anomalies.
- **Immobile Elements**: Ti, Zr, Nb, Y, REE. Crucial for identifying host rock protoliths in intensely altered zones (using Zr/Ti vs Nb/Y plots).

## 4. Orogenic Gold
Structurally controlled, metamorphic fluid-derived gold deposits.

### Alteration Halos
- **Proximal**: Carbonatization (Ankerite, Dolomite), Sericitization, Sulphidation (Pyrite, Arsenopyrite).
- **Geochemistry**: Addition of K, Rb, Cs, CO2, S, As, Au, W, B.
- **Host Rock Control**:
    - **Iron-rich hosts (BIF, Dolerite)**: Desulphidation of fluid precipitates Au (Au-As-S association).
    - **Sedimentary hosts**: Carbonaceous matter acts as reductant.

### Pathfinder Elements
- **Primary**: Au, Ag.
- **Pathfinders**: As, Sb, W, Bi, Te, B.
- **Ratios**: Au/Ag typically high (>5:1). Low base metals (Cu, Pb, Zn) compared to VMS/Epithermal.

## 5. Magmatic Ni-Cu-PGE
Segregation of immiscible sulphide liquids from mafic/ultramafic magmas.

### Komatiite-Hosted Ni
- **Host**: High MgO (>18%) ultramafic flows (Spinifex texture).
- **Geochemistry**:
    - **MgO vs Ni**: Strong positive correlation (Olivine control).
    - **Ore Signature**: High Ni, Cu, Co, PGE.
    - **S/Se Ratio**: Mantle range (2000-4000). High S/Se (>5000) indicates crustal contamination (sediment assimilation), a trigger for sulphide saturation.
    - **PGE Depletion**: Depletion of PGEs (Pt, Pd) in overlying basalts indicates early sulphide segregation (potential ore at depth).

### Intrusion-Hosted
- **Signature**: Cu-Ni-PGE enrichment.
- **Depletion**: Chalcophile element (Ni, Cu, PGE) depletion in host silicate rocks indicates sulphide segregation.

## 6. Carbonatites (REE-Nb-Ta)
Magmatic carbonate rocks, primary source of LREE and Nb.

### Geochemical Signature
- **Enriched**: LREE (La, Ce, Pr, Nd), Nb, Ta, P, Ba, Sr, F.
- **Pathfinders**: Nb (Pyrochlore), P (Apatite), Zr/Hf ratios.
- **Fractionation**: Steep REE patterns (High La/Yb).
- **Fenitization**: Alkali metasomatism of wall rocks.
    - **Sodic Fenite**: Na-amphiboles, pyroxenes. High Na, Fe.
    - **Potassic Fenite**: K-feldspar, biotite. High K, Rb, Ba.
    - **Halo**: Nb and REE anomalies can extend into fenitized zones.

## 7. Unconformity-Related Uranium
High-grade U deposits at the contact between sandstone and basement.

### Alteration Halos
- **Sandstone**: Illite, Chlorite (Sudoite), Dravite (Mg-Tourmaline), Quartz dissolution (desilicification).
- **Basement**: Chlorite, Illite.
- **Geochemistry**:
    - **Core**: U, Pb (radiogenic), Ni, Co, As.
    - **Halo**: B (Dravite), Mg (Chlorite), LREE (up to 500m above ore).
    - **Isotopes**: Low $\delta^{11}B$ in tourmaline (marine brine source).

### Pathfinders
- **Primary**: U.
- **Pathfinders**: Pb isotopes ($^{206}Pb/^{204}Pb$), Ni, Co, As, Cu, Mo, B, REE.
- **Vector**: Increasing Mg/K ratio towards ore (Illite -> Chlorite transition).
